Short answer
Yes — HDFC ERGO’s Optima Secure plan covers AYUSH treatment, but only under specific conditions.

Details / key conditions
- What is covered: Medical expenses for inpatient care under AYUSH systems — Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha and Homeopathy (i.e., only when the insured is admitted as an in‑patient).  
- What is NOT covered: AYUSH treatment taken on an outpatient basis is excluded.  
- Where treatment must be taken: Treatment must be in a registered AYUSH hospital/facility that meets the policy criteria (examples shown in the policy):  
  - at least 5 in‑patient beds;  
  - a qualified/registered AYUSH medical practitioner in charge round the clock;  
  - dedicated AYUSH therapy sections and/or an equipped operation theatre where surgical procedures are performed;  
  - maintains daily patient records and makes them available to the insurer’s authorized representative.  
- Claim handling: Claims are normally on reimbursement; cashless may be considered on a case‑by‑case basis (pre‑authorization required). Standard claim documents (completed claim form, hospital registration certificate/number, discharge summary, final hospital bill with receipts, ID/age proof, previous consultation papers etc.) must be submitted.  
- Other policy controls that apply: waiting periods, pre‑existing disease exclusions, other standard policy exclusions and limits apply as per the policy wording.

Recommendation
Before treatment or at the time of hospitalization, confirm with HDFC ERGO / the TPA whether the hospital is acceptable for AYUSH inpatient claims and whether cashless approval is possible. Also review the exact terms in your policy schedule and the Optima Secure policy wording for any plan‑specific limits, waiting periods or deductibles.
